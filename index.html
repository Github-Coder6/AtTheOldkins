Welcome to the launch of

At the Oldkins

 Previous Next
At the Oldkins written by Apoorva Vishwanath stands out for its uniqueness and simplicity in the children's fiction genre. The language is laudable and is surely a treat to the reader. This is the story of an ordinary girl with little dreams but with a plethora of problems. Nova Pelzer and her friends have just entered middle school. Everything around her begins to turn chaotic. She has to battle between misunderstandings, the pressure of school and amidst all that, a birthday to celebrate.  When her friends want to break a hundred rules with her, the school gives her the responsibility of organising the biggest event of the year. To add to the mix, the four friends have a mystery to solve and save the school.  Will she and her friends be successful? Welcome to Oldkins. Enjoy the thrilling ride!

Reviews

Journey through the book, by StoriesbyChildren

It's been a few months that Apoorva has been with Stories By Children. But in these few months, her little world of imagination is wonderfully showcased in her work as she has submitted a plethora of writings from poetry to stories and theme writing submissions as well. She has written amazingly well and has bagged some awards for her fabulous work. She seems more inclined towards poetry and her poetic nature is seen even in her stories. At the Oldkins is, no doubt, yet another masterpiece by Apoorva which stands out for its uniqueness and simplicity in the fiction genre. She has very beautifully elaborated on the turmoil of a 6th grader and how life opens up to her. Stories By Children wishes her all the luck on the journey she is about to begin.
